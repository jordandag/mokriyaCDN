FROM THE MOUNTAINS AND TRIBES OF SIDAMA TO YOUR CUP

Whats Makes Our Coffee Unique

Completely organic coffee from a complete self-sustained and environment friendly farm!

Everything and everyone in our farm is mutually interconnected. From the water that flows from the mountains and the sunlight that shines down on the highlands to the coffee we produce and the dedicated people who maintain the farm. The river from the mountain sustains the coffee trees and is used to wash the coffee beans during the harvest season. The peeld coffee husks and the dung from the livestock in our farm will go back to the soil and act as a fertlizer to the coffee trees and other plants that sustain and act as a food to our livestock and workers in the farm. Even the drying racks for the coffee beans are all handmade from the bamboo grove behind the farm. Beside cutting off unnecessary expences, this makes our coffee farm throughly self-sustained and organic

The River Beside Our Site

This river from the nearby mountains sustains the life in our farm and is used to clean the coffee

The Livestocks IN Our Site

Their milk and meat is used to feed the workrs in the farm and their dung is used as a fertlizer.

Ensete Trees In Our Site

The Ensete trees(big leaf tree in the picture) root is delicious staple food of the Sidama tribes. Our workers eat it with the milk they get from the livestocks in the site

Enjoying Ensete With Milk

A worker eating processed Ensete root with butter and drinking milk that comes from from the livestock in our site

The Bamboo treas In Our Site

The bamboo trees in our site are used to make the coffee drying racks and also act as a fence to the site.

Bamboo Racks

The Coffee drying racks made from bamboo and other trees in the site are stored to be used during the harvest season. Once a Tree is cutted it can be used for about 5 years. This gives time reforestation we undertake every year.

Husk From The Coffee Beans

The Husk that comes off during the cleansing of the coffee beans is used stacked and used as a fertlizer in our site and near by farmers that supply coffee to us.

The Coffee Growing Village of Sidamo

The morning sun is shining down on the mountainous coffee growing village of Sidamo. Our site is located at the heart of this village

Site Worker Showing Hot Peppers
