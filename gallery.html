This river from the nearby mountains sustains the life in our farm and is used to clean the coffee

Their milk and meat is used to feed the workrs in the farm and their dung is used as a fertlizer.

The Ensete trees(big leaf tree in the picture) root is delicious staple food of the Sidama tribes. Our workers eat it with the milk they get from the livestocks in the site

A worker eating processed Ensete root with butter and drinking milk that comes from from the livestock in our site

The bamboo trees in our site are used to make the coffee drying racks and also act as a fence to the site.

The Coffee drying racks made from bamboo and other trees in the site are stored to be used during the harvest season. Once a Tree is cutted it can be used for about 5 years. This gives time reforestation we undertake every year.

The Husk that comes off during the cleansing of the coffee beans is used stacked and used as a fertlizer in our site and near by farmers that supply coffee to us.

The morning sun is shining down on the mountainous coffee growing village of Sidamo. Our site is located at the heart of this village

Beside peppers we grow maize, collard greens and all sorts of vegitables that will feed the workers in the site year round
